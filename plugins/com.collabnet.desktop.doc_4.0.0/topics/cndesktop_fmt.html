Field mappings

Field mappings are rules that specify how the connector transforms data from one system to the other.

Let's say you have company-wide mapping rules. You can create a field mapping based on those rules and templatize it. All field mappings linked to the template will change whenever you update the template. You may also have special requirements for a particular field mapping that's an exception -- you want it to follow the rules of the template except for a couple of specific fields. In this case, you can create the field mapping based on the template and then customize it for those two fields.

  • Create a field mapping from a template
    You set up field mappings to define how the data in one connector participant is transformed to the other.
  • Templatize a field mapping
    You can establish the scope of your mapping rules at two levels -- by setting up field mapping templates at the connector level or project level.
  • Edit a field mapping
    When you edit an active field mapping that's linked to a template, you can make changes that only affect that mapping or make changes that affect the template to which it is linked.
  • Switch the active field mapping
    When mapping requirements change, you can switch the active field mapping to a different one.
  • Export a field mapping template
    After you've tested your connector on a stage system, export the field mapping templates so that they can be used later in production.
  • Import a field mapping template
    When you're ready to move your connector to a production system, import the field mapping templates your exported from your stage setup.
  • Reverse field mapping rules
    When you've designed field mapping rules for one direction, use the reverse mapping wizard to generate the rules for the opposite direction.
